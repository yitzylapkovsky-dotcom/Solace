💙 You are not alone
What you're feeling matters, and help is available right now — for free, confidentially, 24/7. Please reach out to one of these resources.
📞 Call 988 — Suicide & Crisis Lifeline 💬 Text HOME to 741741 — Crisis Text Line
— or if in immediate danger —
🚨 Call 911 — Emergency Services
📋 Steps to get help right now
1
Call or text 988. A trained counselor answers 24/7, free of charge. You can remain anonymous.
2
If you feel unsafe right now, call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room. Tell them "I am having thoughts of suicide" or "I am having a mental health crisis."
3
Tell someone you trust — a friend, family member, or anyone nearby — that you need support right now.
4
Remove access to means if you can — put distance between yourself and anything you might use to harm yourself.
5
After the immediate crisis, ask your doctor or the 988 counselor to help you find an ongoing therapist or psychiatrist near you.
🏥Find a Mental Health Professional
Ask your primary care doctor for a referral, or visit SAMHSA's National Helpline: 1-800-662-4357 — free referrals to local treatment facilities.
🌐Online Therapy Options
If you can't see someone in person right now, platforms like BetterHelp, Talkspace, and Open Path Collective offer affordable online therapy with licensed therapists.
Solace is a wellness support tool, not a crisis service.
